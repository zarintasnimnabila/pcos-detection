What is PCOS?

PCOS stands for Polycystic Ovary Syndrome. It is a common hormonal disorder that affects women and people assigned female at birth (AFAB) of reproductive age. PCOS is characterized by a combination of symptoms, including irregular menstrual periods, excess androgen hormones (male hormones) in the body, and the presence of multiple small cysts on the ovaries.

The exact cause of PCOS is unknown, but it is believed to involve a combination of genetic and environmental factors. Insulin resistance, which affects the body's ability to use insulin effectively, is also commonly associated with PCOS.
